DIS-358 — Free 1yr historical data for yearly plans

Offer free import of one full year of historical data to Trial users at the Trial & Purchase stages, in exchange for switching from monthly to a yearly plan. The prototype below explores three promotional surfaces — one pricing badge and two in-app banners with a 24h countdown — that carry that offer to the user.

Interactive mock
Pricing badge + in-app offer banners

Toggle between three surfaces: pricing-page badge, banner above the app header, and banner sitting between the trial banner and the header — all with a live 24h countdown.

1. What this idea is about

Offer "Free 1 year of historical import" for Trial users with less than 1M annual revenue, at the Trial & Purchase stages, to increase conversion to Yearly plans. The idea is framed in the Jira card as one of the measures for higher conversion of PLG traffic to yearly.

Source: DIS-358 §1 "The Idea (1 sentence)".

2. Who is the target

PLG users with annual revenue under 1M, who are NOT Accountants in Practice. The card lists both new and existing users under "Affects".

Estimated reach: ~54% of monthly Purchase events are made by <1M ARR users (source: Mixpanel report linked in card §2). Additional eligibility scoping: available only if the user subscribes to a yearly plan today, within the first 24 hours; verification with Val and Aris is still pending per the card.

3. End goal(s)

  • Primary strategic anchor: New Yearly MRR — moves both the Monthly/Yearly purchase ratio and New MRR (card §5).
  • Secondary: churn ↓ in the long run — approximated via secondary signals (card §5).
  • Falsifiable success signal: ratio Purchase Yearly / Purchase overall, direction ↑, target 50%+ (fluctuation fine) — measurable today via Mixpanel (card §9 + top of card).

4. Mechanism & logic

The causal chain, quoted from card §3 "Mechanism (Causality Test)":

  1. If we do free 1 year of historical import for Yearly Subscription…
  2. Then the yearly plan will look more attractive to new PLG users…
  3. Which causes the number of yearly subscriptions to move.

The card explicitly lists two assumptions that have to hold for the chain not to collapse:

  • Users need historical import for that long (i.e. a full year, not less).
  • Users are fine with paying yearly within a limited time window (24 hours).

Source: DIS-358 §3 "Mechanism (Causality Test)".
