DIS-358 — Free 1yr historical data for yearly plans

Trial users on Basic & Essential see a time-boxed offer: subscribe to a yearly plan and get 1 full year of historical data imported for free. The mock explores three promotional surfaces — a pricing badge and two in-app banners with a 24h countdown — to lift PLG yearly conversion without changing the core plan structure.

Interactive mock
Pricing badge + in-app offer banners

Toggle between three surfaces: pricing-page badge, banner above the app header, and banner sitting between the trial banner and the header — all with a live 24h countdown.

1. What this idea is about

Give first-time trial users a concrete, quantifiable reason to pick yearly over monthly: free import of one full year of historical transactions — an add-on that today costs $30 per 500 transactions and is a real friction point for accountants who need past periods synced. The offer is scoped to Basic & Essential (the PLG plans) and gated behind a 24-hour window from trial start, so the incentive doubles as an activation urgency lever.

2. Who is the target

PLG trial users on Basic or Essential — the plans this experiment targets for higher yearly conversion.

Eligibility as scoped in the Jira card: annual revenue up to 1M, no AIPs, within the first 24 hours of trial, subscribing to a yearly plan.

3. End goal(s)

  • Primary metric: Yearly / Total purchase ratio ↑ — target 50%+ (fluctuation fine), per the Jira card. Tracked in the two linked Mixpanel reports on the card.
  • Downstream: LTV lift — yearly cohorts have shown lower churn than monthly in earlier snapshots, so a shifted cohort mix compounds retention.
  • Guardrail: churn on newly-converted yearly subs must not spike — a promo that pulls in wrong-fit users would erode the gain via refunds.
  • Second-order: softens historical-data support load (already sanity-checked with Renata) by resolving the ask up-front instead of via ticket.

4. Mechanism & logic

  1. Trigger. New trial starts → a 24h clock starts. Only Basic/Essential yearly plans qualify for the free-history unlock.
  2. Surface layer. User sees the offer in up to three places (each tested independently in the mock):
    • Pricing page badge — a green pill under the "Pay yearly" toggle: "Includes 1 year of historical data for free — Subscribe Now!" with the countdown. Cheapest surface, catches anyone who reaches pricing.
    • In-app banner above header — full-width promo strip on the transactions page, permanent and center-aligned, with the countdown timer. Maximum visibility.
    • In-app banner below existing trial banner — sits between the standing "0 days trial" banner and the app header. Stacks with existing UI without displacing it — safer roll-out.
  3. Conversion path. Any CTA click deep-links to checkout with yearly Basic/Essential pre-selected and the free-history flag attached to the subscription record.
  4. Fulfillment. On subscription, backend enqueues a historical import job spanning the prior 12 months, capped to the plan's transaction limit. Support handles the same as a paid import; the only difference is the invoice line item.
  5. Why yearly-only. Free history has to be recouped somewhere. Yearly's built-in 12-month commitment gives Synder a full retention window to earn back the import cost. Monthly buyers could churn after month 1 with a year of imported data, so they're excluded from the promo (they see the standard "Paid unlimited history" line).
  6. Why 24 hours. Loss-aversion is stronger than gain-seeking; a visible countdown converts the choice from "should I upgrade?" into "am I willing to lose this?" — and prevents indefinite deferral of the switch decision.
Open questions ahead of challenge: (1) is the 24h window supported by activation-funnel data, or arbitrary? (2) do <1M ARR trial users actually ask for 12 months of history, or is that mostly an AIP/mid-market ask? (3) SLG bleed-through tripwire — what % of free-history claims by SLG-eligible accounts would trigger a rethink?
